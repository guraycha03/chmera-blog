    Websites I Use Every Day That Actually Make Life Easier

    Web Development & Design • by Charisse Guray • Sept 07, 2025

    I’ve come across a lot of websites over time, but only a few have stuck with me as true go-to tools. They’re not just “cool finds” I saved once and forgot about — these are the ones I actually return to almost every day. Some spark design ideas, some help me fix things quickly, and others just make the process a whole lot smoother. Here’s a roundup of the sites I personally rely on:

    Design Tools & Inspiration

    • Toools.design – A free collection of design resources, templates, and handy UI links. I often check it out when I’m stuck on layouts and need a fresh spark.
    • Uiverse – Think of it like a library of ready-to-use components (buttons, cards, loaders). They’re free, customizable, and copy-paste ready — perfect for quick experiments.
    • Land-book – Whenever I’m hunting for landing page ideas, this is my first stop. It’s basically a gallery of beautiful, real-world web designs.

    Images & Illustrations

    • Pexels – 100% free stock photos and videos. The quality is surprisingly good, and I’ve used it everywhere from blog posts to mockups.
    • Pixabay – Similar to Pexels, also free. I like having both bookmarked because sometimes I find what I need here when Pexels doesn’t have it.
    • UnDraw – Free vector illustrations that you can even customize by color before downloading. They blend well in modern, minimalist designs.

    Image Editing & Optimization

    • Cleanup.pictures – Magic eraser for photos. I use it to remove text, logos, or random objects in just seconds. Free, with a paid upgrade if you want higher resolution.
    • Remove.bg – The fastest way I’ve found to remove backgrounds from images. It’s free for basic use, and the accuracy is usually spot-on.
    • Cloudinary – My go-to for hosting and optimizing images. It automatically compresses, resizes, and delivers images fast — very useful for personal projects or GitHub demos.

    Icons & SVGs

    • Flaticon – Tons of free and premium icons in every style you can think of. I usually grab the free ones and tweak them.
    • Iconfinder – Similar to Flaticon but with a different collection. Great for variety.
    • SVG Repo – Completely free SVGs that are easy to edit. Super handy for projects where you need lightweight icons.

    Colors & AI Tools

    • Colors.artyclick – Not only shows you palettes, but also gives exact color names. I find it helpful when I want my palette to sound professional, not just “blue-ish.”
    • Meta AI – A free AI tool I use to generate realistic images. It’s super handy for creating fake user profile pictures, review images, or even product shots for my projects. Saves me from spending hours searching for the “perfect” stock photo.

    That’s my little toolbox. Some of these are lifesavers when I need quick solutions, and others are just fun to explore. If you’ve got favorite websites like these, I’d love to hear about them — I’m always looking for more gems to add to my daily routine.
