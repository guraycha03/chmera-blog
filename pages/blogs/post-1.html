    5 Productivity Tools Every Student Should Try

    Student Life • by Charisse Guray • Sept 13, 2025

    Being a student comes with a lot of responsibilities and deadlines. Here are five productivity tools that can help you manage your time, stay organized, and get more done efficiently.

    1. Notion

    Notion is an all-in-one workspace where you can write, plan, collaborate, and organize. Many students use it for notes, project management, and habit tracking.

    Pro Tip: Use Notion templates for faster setup and weekly planners.

    2. Trello

    Trello is perfect for organizing tasks and projects visually. Create boards, lists, and cards to manage assignments and deadlines.

    3. Todoist

    A simple but powerful task manager that tracks your daily to-dos. Set priorities, deadlines, and reminders to stay on top of your work.

    4. Google Calendar

    Google Calendar helps you schedule classes, study sessions, and events. Sync across devices and get notifications for deadlines.

    "Time management is not about doing more, it's about doing what matters." — Productivity Quote

    5. Grammarly

    Grammarly is an AI-powered writing assistant. Correct grammar, spelling, and style mistakes in essays, emails, and blog posts.

    Pro Tip: Use Grammarly’s tone detector to match your writing style for blogs or emails.

    Bonus Tips for Students

    • Set clear goals for each week.
    • Break tasks into smaller, manageable chunks.
    • Eliminate distractions when studying.
    • Use a Pomodoro timer for better focus.
    • Review your progress weekly.
